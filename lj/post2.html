Overuse of Overlays

Before reading the NNG's article on overlays, I thought overlays were annoying popups that asked users to subscribe. After reading the article, I learned about where overlays are used, alternatives, and how to make them less intruding.

The article taught me that it's best to keep overlay messages brief and descriptive, with options to easily close the overlay. For ones with an urgent message, this can mean having clear buttons that show what the user is doing will have serious consequences (Ex: deleting something) or so a user can easily submit important information.

Unimportant or long overlay messages should be reconsidered because mobile users or those that need to zoom in will have a hard time viewing it. Safe ways to ensure users see the overlays instead of quickly clicking out is to put them closer to the top of the browser, using alternatives like a separate page or a feature box, or reserving overlays for user-initiated actions (sharing) and urgent messages.
